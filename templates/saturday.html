The Joyful Mysteries

Saturday Rosary

How to Pray Each Mystery

Before each mystery, announce it and then pray the following:

  • 1x Our Father
  • 10x Hail Mary
  • 1x Glory Be

Prayers

Our Father
Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name. Thy kingdom come, thy will be done, on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread, and forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us. And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.

Hail Mary
Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee. Blessed art thou among women, and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus. Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners, now and at the hour of our death. Amen.

Glory Be
Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.

1. The Annunciation

  • The Angel Gabriel appears to Mary
  • Mary is troubled but listens
  • Gabriel tells her she will conceive by the Holy Spirit
  • Mary says, "Be it done unto me according to thy word"
  • Mary’s perfect obedience and humility
  • The Word is made flesh and dwells among us
  • Jesus is conceived in Mary's womb
  • Mary becomes the new Ark of the Covenant
  • Faith begins to be fulfilled in salvation history
  • Mary’s Fiat echoes through eternity

2. The Visitation

  • Mary travels in haste to see Elizabeth
  • Elizabeth is filled with the Holy Spirit
  • "Blessed are you among women"
  • John leaps in the womb at Jesus' presence
  • Mary stays and helps Elizabeth
  • The beauty of serving others in need
  • Mary’s humility in sharing joy and help
  • The power of the unborn Christ
  • The recognition of the Messiah by John
  • The Magnificat is proclaimed

3. The Nativity

  • Jesus is born in Bethlehem
  • Mary lays Him in a manger
  • There is no room in the inn
  • The humility of the Savior's birth
  • Angels proclaim the good news to shepherds
  • Shepherds come to adore the newborn King
  • The Word made flesh dwells among us
  • The Magi begin their journey
  • Peace enters the world through a child
  • Mary treasures all these things in her heart

4. The Presentation

  • Mary and Joseph take Jesus to the temple
  • Jesus is presented according to the Law of Moses
  • Simeon recognizes Jesus as the Messiah
  • "A light of revelation to the Gentiles"
  • Mary is told a sword will pierce her heart
  • The offering of two doves by a humble family
  • Anna the prophetess gives thanks to God
  • Jesus is consecrated to the Lord
  • The fulfillment of the law and prophecy
  • God's promise is recognized in the Infant Christ

5. The Finding in the Temple

  • Jesus goes missing during the return from Jerusalem
  • Mary and Joseph search anxiously for three days
  • They find Him in the temple teaching the elders
  • "Did you not know I must be in my Father's house?"
  • Jesus amazes the teachers with His understanding
  • Mary and Joseph do not fully understand
  • Jesus returns with them and is obedient
  • Mary treasures all these things in her heart
  • The wisdom of the young Christ is revealed
  • The beginning of His public awareness of His mission

Closing Prayers

Hail, Holy Queen
Hail, Holy Queen, Mother of mercy, our life, our sweetness, and our hope. To thee do we cry, poor banished children of Eve. To thee do we send up our sighs, mourning and weeping in this valley of tears. Turn then, most gracious advocate, thine eyes of mercy toward us, and after this our exile, show unto us the blessed fruit of thy womb, Jesus. O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary. Pray for us, O Holy Mother of God, that we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ. Amen.

Sign of the Cross
In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

  • The Visitation: Luke 1:39–56
  • The Nativity: Luke 2:1–20, Matthew 1:18–25
  • The Presentation: Luke 2:22–38
  • The Finding in the Temple: Luke 2:41–52
