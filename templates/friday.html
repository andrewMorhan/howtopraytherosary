The Sorrowful Mysteries

friday Rosary

How to Pray Each Mystery

Before each mystery, announce it and then pray the following:

  • 1x Our Father
  • 10x Hail Mary
  • 1x Glory Be

Prayers

Our Father
Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name. Thy kingdom come, thy will be done, on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread, and forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us. And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.

Hail Mary
Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee. Blessed art thou among women, and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus. Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners, now and at the hour of our death. Amen.

Glory Be
Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.

1. The Agony in the Garden

  • Jesus goes to Gethsemane to pray
  • He asks the disciples to keep watch
  • Jesus is deeply sorrowful, even to death
  • He prays, “Let this cup pass, but not my will”
  • His sweat becomes like drops of blood
  • Angels come to strengthen Him
  • Jesus accepts the Father’s will completely
  • The disciples fall asleep despite His request
  • Judas arrives with the soldiers
  • Jesus is betrayed with a kiss and arrested

2. The Scourging at the Pillar

  • Jesus is brought before Pilate
  • The crowd demands His crucifixion
  • Pilate has Him scourged to appease the crowd
  • Roman soldiers tie Jesus to a pillar
  • He is whipped brutally
  • Each lash tears into His flesh
  • Jesus offers this suffering for our sins
  • The silence of Christ amid torture
  • The humility of the Suffering Servant
  • The injustice borne with love and patience

3. The Crowning with Thorns

  • The soldiers mock Jesus as a king
  • A crown of thorns is twisted together
  • They press it onto His head
  • Blood flows down His face
  • They clothe Him in a purple robe
  • They kneel and strike Him, saying, “Hail, King of the Jews”
  • Jesus silently endures the humiliation
  • The contrast of mockery and true kingship
  • The pain of insult and ridicule
  • The Lamb of God is crowned in suffering

4. The Carrying of the Cross

  • Jesus is given His cross to carry
  • The weight presses down on His torn back
  • He stumbles multiple times
  • Mary meets Him along the way
  • Simon of Cyrene is forced to help
  • Veronica wipes His face
  • The women of Jerusalem weep for Him
  • Jesus tells them to weep for themselves and their children
  • The journey to Calvary continues in pain
  • Jesus perseveres in love and obedience

5. The Crucifixion

  • Jesus is nailed to the cross
  • He prays, “Father, forgive them”
  • Soldiers cast lots for His garments
  • He speaks to the good thief
  • “Today you will be with Me in Paradise”
  • He gives Mary to John as mother
  • Darkness covers the land
  • Jesus cries out and gives up His spirit
  • The veil of the temple is torn
  • The centurion proclaims, “Truly this was the Son of God”

Closing Prayers

Hail, Holy Queen
Hail, Holy Queen, Mother of mercy, our life, our sweetness, and our hope. To thee do we cry, poor banished children of Eve. To thee do we send up our sighs, mourning and weeping in this valley of tears. Turn then, most gracious advocate, thine eyes of mercy toward us, and after this our exile, show unto us the blessed fruit of thy womb, Jesus. O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary. Pray for us, O Holy Mother of God, that we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ. Amen.

Sign of the Cross
In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.
